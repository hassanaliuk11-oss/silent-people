01 · The First Evening

The city stood wrapped in silence, as if even time had forgotten its duty. Lamps glowed without warmth, streets breathed without footsteps, and every window carried a story too heavy to speak aloud.

Hassan watched the world from behind the glass. Faces passed like unfinished sentences. Everyone carried something invisible, something heavier than memory, something only silence could understand.

“Silence is not the absence of sound, but the presence of everything unsaid.”

02 · The Weight of Quiet

Evening arrived gently, not as night but as confession. Doors closed softly, yet hearts remained open and wounded. Words gathered courage — and failed.

Silence became a language of its own. It spoke through pauses, through lowered eyes, through the ache that never learned how to scream.
